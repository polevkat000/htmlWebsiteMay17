Orcas: Orcinus Orca

Fast Facts

    Orcas...

  • gained their common name from sailors, who witnessed them preying on larger whales
  • have a Spanish translation for their common name, ballena asesina, which translates to "assassin whale"

Threats

Common threats to Orcas are often not given enough attention. In the 1900s, whale capturing reached a high. Entertainment companies, such as Sea World, captured Orcas from the wild to train them and perform for the public. In 1976, however, this trend began declining- Sea World agreed to end this capture cycle. Toxic chemicals, leading to lack of food, and the general inavailability of their regular diet, causes a major distress among Orca whale researchers.
